Classic Beef Bourguignon

This traditional French stew is braised in red wine with pearl onions, mushrooms, and bacon lardons. Serves 6. Total time: approximately 3 hours including 2.5 hours of braising.

Ingredients:
- 3 pounds beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
- 6 ounces thick-cut bacon, cut into lardons
- 1 bottle (750ml) dry red Burgundy wine
- 2 cups beef stock
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 18 pearl onions, peeled
- 1 pound cremini mushrooms, quartered
- 3 carrots, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:
Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Pat the beef dry and season generously with salt and pepper. In a large Dutch oven, cook the bacon over medium heat until crisp, about 8 minutes. Remove bacon and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

Working in batches, brown the beef on all sides in the bacon fat, about 3 minutes per side. Do not crowd the pot. Remove browned beef and set aside. Add the carrots and pearl onions to the pot and cook until lightly browned, about 5 minutes.

Return the beef and bacon to the pot. Sprinkle with flour and toss to coat. Add the wine, beef stock, tomato paste, garlic, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a simmer, then cover and transfer to the oven. Braise for 2 to 2.5 hours, until the beef is fork-tender.

During the last 30 minutes of cooking, sauté the mushrooms in butter over high heat until golden. Add the mushrooms to the stew. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve garnished with chopped fresh parsley over egg noodles or mashed potatoes.
