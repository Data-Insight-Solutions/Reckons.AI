# Reckons.AI v0.1.0

The first tagged release of Reckons.AI, a local-first personal knowledge graph that runs
entirely in your browser. Here is an honest summary of what shipped.

## Highlights

- **Local-first knowledge graphs** — Statements live in IndexedDB (via Dexie); no server
  is required. Each graph is a portable, versionable store you can export as Turtle at
  any time.
- **Multi-provider LLM extraction with review** — Claude, OpenAI, Gemini, Ollama,
  OpenRouter, Chrome's built-in AI, or a fully offline WASM model extract facts from
  whatever you ingest. Every extracted statement lands in a review workflow (pending,
  confirmed, refined, rejected, superseded) before it is trusted.
- **2D and 3D graph views** — Explore your knowledge as an interactive force-directed
  graph, with per-entity icons, hierarchy layouts, and 3D models for key nodes.
- **Shelly, the in-app assistant** — A chat companion that can search, explain, and tour
  your graph, including guided stories with text-to-speech playback.
- **Browser extension** — Capture pages as you browse, accumulate a research session
  across tabs, and batch-ingest with at-a-glance conflict and reinforcement summaries.
- **MCP server** — Query your knowledge graphs from Claude Code, Claude Desktop, or any
  MCP client: full-text search, subgraph traversal, AI-grounded reckonings
  (situation to target to proposal), and git-aware alignment checks against a project's
  own roadmap.
- **Cross-KB alignment** — Compare and merge entities across two graphs, with
  embedding-based matching and IRI remapping for the parts you accept.
- **Graph-published docs** — This documentation site is generated from a Reckons.AI
  graph and published through a Sveltia CMS-backed workflow: pages are graph nodes,
  markdown is a generated by-product, and a CI check keeps the two in sync.

## Under the hood

- A content safety preamble is injected into every LLM call, backed by a pattern-based
  classifier that filters disallowed content before it ever reaches your graph.
- BGE-small-en-v1.5 embeddings power semantic search, merge detection, and cross-KB
  entity matching.
- The product's own roadmap, architecture, and test coverage are documented as
  queryable TTL knowledge bases rather than scattered markdown files, with a
  git-aware alignment check to catch drift between code and plan.

## What's next

Progressive enrichment pipelines, a VS Code integration, and enterprise access controls
are on the roadmap. See the [Docs](/docs) for what has shipped and what is still
planned.
